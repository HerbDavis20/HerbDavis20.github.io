Evaluating Bitcoin: The Transition Towards Financial Legitimacy

Aims:


This project aims to provide an insight into how the perception of Bitcoin has changed over time. In it's early years, traditional investors had significant reservations on the validity of Bitcoin due to its erratic price volatility and domination in dark web transactions. Now, Bitcoin is a common asset among some of the largest funds and asset managers.

Through this project I aim to take a data driven approach, using multiple metrics and statistical methods to reveal the changes in the fundamental value of Bitcoin, to both institutional and retail investors.

I thought comparing bitcoin and the VIX would be insightful as high market volatility often pushes investors to move from stocks into 'safe haven' assets like gold or U.S. Treasury bonds. If there is a similar trend with Bitcoin, it implies a shift in bitcoins perception.

Challenges:

Knowing how to filter and sort data to make creating a complex chart in vega more efficint I found to be particularly hard and took a lot of trial and error. Making sure the data was in long form was a good starting point to solve this.

Working with dates caused many issues due to the different formats. To resolve this, I standardised all my timeseries data using pd.to_datetime function on all timeseries data before my analysis.

Another issue I came across was finding live price data. Many of the API's that are available were behind paywalls and only allowed minimal data to be extracted for free. I discovered the yfinance package late into my project but this was an effective tool in sourcing live financial data.

Another challenge I faced was incorectly formatted numbers, causing NaN outputs when analyising. To solve this I made sure to convert all the data into floats or integers using .astype(float)

When filtering data in pandas, I learnt to reset the index to prevent merging issues. Creating seperate dataframes at each stage I also found to make experimenting and finding errors more efficient.


Conclusion:

My analysis supports the evolving perception of Bitcoin, moving from a highly speculative asset to an increasingly stable asset, more traditionally aligned with 'safe haven'assets that investors flock to during times of high market turbulence. Looking into bitcoin's correlation with other traditional assets and the VIX index showed bitcoin increasingly behaving like a risk-off asset, where in its early days it was nothing but a speculative bubble. before 2018 where both correlations are negative suggests a contradiction in bitcoins sentiment. More recently there is a clear divide in the two correlations which shows that bitcoin's perception is consolidating. There is still a way to go for bitcoin however, as when comparing the risk/reward with gold, it is clear to see such variance at times cannot be associated with a low risk asset. Furthermore, in times of deep market stress, investors are looking for assets with intrinsic value, not just a speculation fuelled asset.
